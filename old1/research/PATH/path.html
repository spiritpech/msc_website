Advanced Vehicle Control Systems

The Automated Highway System (AHS) program aims at automating highway driving in California.This lab is involved with developing lateral control algorithms to realise lane keeping and lane changing operations. Work in control system design is done in association with the Vehicle Dynamics Laboratory, UC Berkeley.  Tests are performed on a test track at Richmond Field Station, CA, USA.

(For more general information on the AHS program visit the PATH site)

Current research projects include:

  • Fault Diagnosis and Management in the Extended AHS architecture (Shashikanth S)
    • Inrtoduction to Fault Detection
    • Related work done in the Vehicle Dynamics Lab
  • Lateral Control of Heavy Duty Vehicles for Automated Highway Systems (Pushkar, Jeng-Yu Wang, Meihua Tai)
    • Robust H-infinity Lateral Control for Heavy Vehicle
    • The Heavy Vehicle Group! (Tomi, Pushkar, Meihua, Jeng- Yu)
  • Bus Collision Warning System (Kun Zhou)
  • Modelling human driving (Gunther Prokop)
  • Human-machine interface for assistance of vehicle steering guidance (Kai -Ten Feng)
