Taming Internal Dynamics by Mismatched and H-infinity-Optimized State Observer
  
Soichi Ibaraki and Masayoshi Tomizuka
 
 
Abstract
  
The feedback linearization control scheme is quite effective for control of a single-input single-output (SISO) linear parameter-varying (LPV) plant; it cancels all parameter-dependent terms in the closed-loop input-output dynamics without using any linear approximation. When the relative degree of the plant is less than its order, the feedback linearization control scheme generates the internal dynamics, which may or may not be stable. This paper presents the stabilization methodology of systems with unstable internal dynamics without using an additional outer-loop controller. The feedback linearization controller requires a state observer. When the Luenberger state observer is applied, the internal dynamics is completely preserved in the overall closed-loop system. By making the choice of system matrices of the state observer completely open, we can search for the observer structure that not only provides the controller with good estimates of the plant state variables, but also stabilizes the entire closed-loop system. Standard H-infinity controller synthesis algorithms can be applied to such a search. Two application examples, including application to steering control for lateral motion of heavy-duty vehicles, are presented to illustrate how the proposed mismatched observer stabilizes the entire closed-loop system.
