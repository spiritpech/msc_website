Data Storage Systems

The date storage industry integrates technology from several areas, most notably electronics, magnetics, materials, and mechanics. Products include magnetic disk drives, optical disk drives, and tape drives. The current industry trend is to continue to refine the existing technologies. A key aspect of this refinement is the reduction of the physical size of drive systems. As the size is reduced and the number of portable applications increases, the robustness of the drive system becomes increasingly important.

As the size decreases, the performance of the servo system must increase to accommodate the more dense track spacing. Futhermore, the drive must maintain a rapid seek and settle performance. To increase tracking servo performance, novel methods for position encoding and detection are current research areas. Control methods such as the use of robust design tools, algorithms for runout cancellation, self-calibration and tuning, and disturbance sensing and rejection are current topics. For larger systems such as tape drives and disk arrays, control techniques for multi-input and multi-output systems and control algorithm for motion synchronization hold promice.

Our group has been or is currently investigating several of these control techniques. This work is in collaboration with the Computer Mechanics Laboratory within the Mechanical Engineering Department, which addresses mechanical issues in data storage devices.
