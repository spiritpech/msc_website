MULTIRATE CONTROLLER FOR HARD DISK DRIVE

Takeyori Hara

PROJECT DESCRIPTION

Recent growth in the track density of hard disk drives has required higher controller bandwidth. On the other hand, the paradigm of servo format methods has shifted from dedicated servo to embedded servo even in high-end disk drives which have many (4 to 10) disks. This causes a tradeoff problem between sampling rate and date format efficiency. The multirate estimation-output controller is one approach to achieve higher control bandwidth under lower sampling frequency by updating the input signal at a higher frequency than the sampling frequency for the tracking error. The input signal is updated based on an estimated state between two sampling instances. The multi-rate ratio (MRR) is defined by (Input Update Frequency)/(Output Sampling Frequency). The method has been successfully implemented in some commercial products. However, further analysis is required for future disk drives in particular for increased MRR implementations. This project will examine advantage and theoretical limit of multirate controller, and develop design and implementation method suitable for the actual drives.

METHODOLOGY

  • Implement multirate controller in a disk drive and compare with high and low frequency single-rate controller.
  • Simulate frequency response of an ideal multirate controller and compare with actual drive.
  • Evaluate the influence of time delay and develop a method to minimize its influence.
  • Evaluate the influence of model error/perturbation.
  • Develop a auto tuning method for estimator coefficients to minimize the model error.

RESULTS TO DATE

  • Complete a hardware setup and tested a multirate control scheme with MRR=4.
  • Compared with single-rate controller in magnitude of tracking run-out and frequency response.
  • Simulated frequency response and compared with experimental results.
